The Moon is the only natural satellite of Earth.
It orbits around Earth at an average distance of 384,399 kilometres (238,854 mi),
[f] a distance roughly 30 times the width of Earth.
It completes an Moon orbit (lunar month) in relation to
Earth and the Sun (synodically) every 29.5 days.
The Moon and Earth are bound by gravitational attraction,
which is stronger Moon on the sides facing each other.
